About the Hackathon

Learning the Earth with AI and Physics (LEAP) is a National Science Foundation - Science and Technology Center that brings together researchers, city agencies, community organizations, and data practitioners around shared urban challenges. The January 2026 hackathon focused on climate resilience: the capacity of New York's neighborhoods to withstand, adapt to, and recover from compounding climate stressors.

Over three days, fifteen teams worked across five neighborhoods — each selected for its distinctive climate risk profile and community context. Teams had access to JupyterHub computing environments pre-loaded with curated datasets, and were supported by mentors from city agencies, academic institutions, and the private sector.

The central thesis: that meaningful climate solutions emerge not from siloed expertise, but from genuine collaboration across disciplinary and community boundaries. The projects showcased here reflect what becomes possible when a data scientist works alongside a community organizer and an urban planner — each bringing irreplaceable knowledge to the table.

This website documents the methodology, the projects, and the data infrastructure that made the hackathon possible — so that future cohorts, partner cities, and the public can learn from and build on this work.

01

Neighborhood Selection

Five neighborhoods were selected to represent diverse climate risk profiles — coastal flooding, urban heat, stormwater, and compound exposures — across all five boroughs.

02

Team Formation

Teams were intentionally cross-disciplinary, pairing technical practitioners with community experts. No team was composed entirely of data scientists.

03

Data Infrastructure

JupyterHub environments pre-loaded with NYC Open Data, federal datasets, and neighborhood-specific layers gave every team a common starting point without requiring data wrangling from scratch.

04

Evaluation

Projects were evaluated on methodological rigor, actionability for city agencies and community organizations, and quality of cross-disciplinary collaboration demonstrated in the work.

05

Ongoing Access

Winning teams received continued JupyterHub access to develop their projects further. All project code and documentation is available in the shared repository.
